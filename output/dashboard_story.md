# Dashboard Story — Executive Sales Performance Review

**Prepared for:** Retail Leadership Team  
**Prepared by:** Jahnavi Gurram  
**Period:** January 2024 – December 2025

---

## Executive Summary

The retail chain generated ₹21.7 Cr in total sales and ₹3.33 Cr in profit across 4,200 orders over two years, achieving an overall profit margin of 15.3%. While topline growth is healthy, three areas demand immediate attention: the disproportionate return rate in Furniture (7.7% vs 3% in Technology), the steep margin erosion at discount levels above 25%, and the underperformance of the East region relative to its return burden.

---

## What Is Performing Well

The business has strong foundational performance across several dimensions. The South region leads with ₹6.47 Cr in sales and maintains healthy profitability, followed closely by North. Technology is the profit engine of the business — at ₹2.8 Cr in profit, it contributes 84% of total profit while maintaining an 18% margin across its sub-categories. Copiers, Accessories, and Phones are the top three sub-categories, each generating over ₹70 lakh in profit.

Customer segments are remarkably balanced — Home Office (₹7.45 Cr), Consumer (₹7.19 Cr), and Corporate (₹7.06 Cr) contribute nearly equally, which reduces dependency risk on any single segment. The Organic campaign channel drives 41% of all sales without paid acquisition costs, making it the most efficient channel.

Monthly trends show consistent peaks in July-August and October-November, suggesting predictable seasonal patterns the business can plan around.

---

## What Is Underperforming

Furniture has the highest return rate at 7.7% — more than double the rate of Technology (3.0%). This is not just a customer satisfaction problem; each returned Furniture order typically involves high-value items (tables, chairs), making the revenue impact significant. Within Furniture, Chairs and Tables drive the bulk of this return volume.

Office Supplies contributes the least to both sales (₹1.15 Cr) and profit (₹17 lakh). While individual sub-categories like Labels and Binders are low-margin by nature, the entire category operates at thin margins without sufficient volume to compensate.

April and June consistently show sales dips across both years, with June 2025 recording only 140 orders — the lowest monthly count in the dataset. This pattern suggests either a seasonal slowdown or a gap in promotional activity during these months.

---

## What Risks Are Visible

**Discount-driven margin erosion is the most significant risk.** Orders with discounts above 25% have an average profit margin of only 6.5%, compared to 19.8% for orders with discounts under 5%. The business is offering high discounts on 592 orders (14% of total), and the incremental volume may not justify the margin sacrifice. Without a clear correlation between heavy discounting and customer retention, this pattern risks training customers to wait for sales.

**Furniture returns at 7.7%** represent both a margin risk and a customer experience risk. High return rates in physical products typically indicate either quality issues, misleading product descriptions, or delivery damage — all of which have different solutions.

**Standard Class shipping**, used for 58% of all orders, averages 4.7 days for delivery. In a market where fast delivery is increasingly expected, this could be contributing to customer dissatisfaction — the return rate for Standard Class orders should be cross-checked to confirm.

---

## What Opportunities Are Visible

1. **South and West regions** show strong performance and could absorb increased marketing investment, particularly through Organic and Paid channels which have proven ROI.

2. **Technology upselling** — the high margin and low return rate make Technology an ideal focus for cross-sell and upsell campaigns, especially Copiers and Accessories where margins exceed 18%.

3. **Seasonal planning** — the predictable July-August and Oct-Nov peaks present an opportunity to pre-stock inventory and increase marketing spend ahead of these periods.

4. **Same Day and First Class shipping** are underutilized (only 871 orders combined vs 2,435 for Standard Class). Promoting faster shipping options could improve customer satisfaction and reduce return rates.

---

## Recommended Business Actions

1. **Cap discounts at 20% for most categories** — margins drop sharply beyond this point. Reserve 25%+ discounts for clearance-only situations and track the volume impact carefully.

2. **Investigate Furniture returns** — commission a root cause analysis (quality audit, packaging review, product description accuracy check). A 2-percentage-point reduction in Furniture returns would save an estimated ₹15-20 lakh annually.

3. **Increase marketing in April and June** — these are consistent dip months. Targeted campaigns or seasonal promotions could smooth the revenue curve.

4. **Shift shipping mix** — incentivize First Class shipping for high-value orders. The 1.8-day average delivery is a strong differentiator and may reduce returns caused by delivery delays.

5. **Double down on Organic channel** — it drives 41% of sales with no paid acquisition cost. Investing in SEO, content, and organic visibility is likely to deliver the highest ROI.

---

## Limitations of the Dashboard

- The data covers only 2 years — not enough to establish long-term trends or distinguish seasonal patterns from one-time events.
- Customer lifetime value is not captured — we cannot tell if heavily discounted customers return for full-price purchases later.
- The return_flag is binary (yes/no) — we don't know the reason for returns (quality, fit, damage, changed mind).
- Campaign channel attribution is single-touch — a customer may have been exposed to multiple channels before purchasing.
- 24 records have missing campaign_channel and 32 have missing customer_rating — minor but worth noting.

---

## Suggested Next Analysis

- Customer cohort analysis to measure retention rates by acquisition channel and segment
- Return reason categorization to separate quality issues from logistics problems
- Price elasticity analysis to find the optimal discount level by category
- Shipping mode vs return rate cross-tabulation to determine if faster shipping reduces returns
